# Meet the Royal Families of AmbonMUD

AmbonMUD is a text-based multiplayer game (a "MUD") built in Kotlin — a
modern take on the classic genre with ANSI color, WebSocket support, NPC
behavior trees, quests, crafting, and sprawling YAML-driven worlds. Among
those worlds are two zones very close to my heart: **PBrae** and
**Wesleyalis**, kingdoms inspired by my four niblings.

---

## Castle PBrae — The Kingdom of King Peanut & Queen Braelynn

Castle PBrae is a whimsical royal fortress ruled jointly by **King Peanut**
and **Queen Braelynn**, named after my youngest niblings.

King Peanut's domain is a love letter to his favorite games. His dream world
features **Peanut's Rocket Arena** (inspired by Rocket League), a **Nether
fortress** crawling with Creepers, and **Peanut's Olympus** — a mountaintop
littered with godly treasure. His Pixel Throne Room is an assembly of pixel
art, rocket car parts, and Minecraft blocks, because why choose one aesthetic
when you can have them all?

Queen Braelynn's side of the castle is a completely different vibe. She reigns
from a throne constructed entirely of interlocking penguin plushies atop the
**Rainbow Obby Tower**. Her kingdom includes the **Penguin Palace** — an ice
palace where penguins wear formal attire — the **Stuffie Grove**, a sacred
forest where felt-heart leaves shelter armies of stuffed animals, and
**Braelynn's Roblox Plaza**, complete with the Adopt Me! Ranch. Her Royal
Goblet sits on her throne, and she has dozens of penguin plushies, though one
is clearly special.

Connecting their kingdoms is the **Royal Chicken Coop**, a grid of rooms
housing hens, turkeys, ducks, a magnificent Royal Rooster, and the Sacred
Royal Egg hidden in the Egg Vault.

## The Wesleyalis Treehouse — Kingdom of King Wesley & Princess Aurora

The Wesleyalis zone is set in a prehistoric rainforest where a magical
treehouse serves as the shared domain of **King Wesley the Artist** and
**Princess Aurora**, named after my older niblings.

King Wesley's kingdom is a living art gallery. Paintings shift and change on
the walls. Pixel-art sprites drift through the air. His throne room is part
gallery, part studio, and his **Gallery of Living Art** features paintings
that literally step out of their frames. Deep in his domain lurks **Godzilla,
King of the Monsters** — a Level 15 boss with 5,000 HP that emerges from
Wesley's imagination with iconic roars. His signature item is the **Enchanted
Art Brush**, whose bristles shimmer with every color ever mixed and fill you
with an overwhelming urge to create.

Princess Aurora's kingdom is... a study in contrasts. Her **Stuffie
Wonderland** is a serene sanctuary of named stuffed animals organized by
species and emotional significance, watched over by her beloved **Rainbow
Stuffed Unicorn** (one ear slightly flattened from years of hugging). But
venture too far and you'll find the **Tantrum Wasteland**, where toddler
displeasure has scattered stuffies across the floor, emptied bookshelves, and
left bold crayon patterns on every surface. Her **Glitter Crown** — cardboard
dusted in silver and gold — gets on everything it touches, which feels right.

Below the treehouse, a prehistoric jungle teems with velociraptors,
triceratops, pterodactyls, and a full apiary with worker bees and a Queen
Bee. And in the basement? The **Servants' Quarters**, where the Royal Servant
Parents manage the impossible task of running "2 KINGDOMS, 1 TREEHOUSE, 1
PREHISTORIC APIARY, MISC. DINOSAURS."

## Why Build This?

These zones aren't just game content — they're portraits. Every room, item,
and NPC captures something real about these four kids: Peanut's obsession with
Rocket League, Braelynn's penguin collection, Wesley's art, Aurora's stuffed
unicorn with the flat ear. Building them into a living, explorable world felt
like the best kind of gift — one where you can wander through someone's
imagination and find a Sacred Royal Egg in a chicken coop, or fight Godzilla
in an art studio, or sit on a throne made of penguins.

The zones were part of AmbonMUD's development and are available as optional
content outside the base release.
